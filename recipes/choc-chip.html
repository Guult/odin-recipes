Gooey Chocolate Chip Cookies

If you are a fan of Penn Station's chocolate chip cookies, with huge chunks of chocolate chips, then you'll love this recipe. This recipe uses dark chocolate and semi sweet chocolate chips to give it that perfect balance of sweet and bitter. The taste is of a rich buttery cookie with solid hits of bitter dark chocolate. If dark chocolate isn't your favorite don't be put off as the sugar in the cookie balances this out perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 3/4C unsalted butter, melted and cooled just until opaque
  • 1C brown sugar
  • 1/2C granulated sugar
  • 1Tbsp vanilla extract
  • 1 egg, room temperature
  • 1 egg yolk, room temperature
  • 2 1/3C of all purpose flour
  • 1tsp baking soda
  • 1Tbsp corn starch
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 100g 70% dark chocolate
  • 100g semi sweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Whisk together melted butter, brown sugar and granulated sugar in a large bowl for two minutes.
  2. Whisk in the vanilla extract, egg and egg yolk until the mixture is light and creamy.
  3. Add the flour, baking soda, corn starch, and salt mixing until just combined.
  4. Stir in dark chocolate and chocolate chips
  5. Scoop out cookie dough, about 1/4 cup per cookie, and roll the dough balls in your hands until smooth. Place on the parchment lined baking sheet and freeze for 30 to 60 minutes.
  6. Preheat oven to 325 degrees.
  7. Place cookie dough about 3 inches apart on parchment lined baking sheet or a baking sheet with a silicone baking mat.
  8. Bake for 12 to 15 minutes in the preheated oven. The cookies are done when the edges are golden and the center looks slightly under baked.
  9. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for at least 5 minutes and then transfer to wire racks to finish cooling.
  10. Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days.
